Laos

Specifications

  • Local Name: ລາວ (Lao)
  • Proportion: 2:3
  • Name of the Flag: ທຸງຊາດລາວ Thong dwang deaen (White Moon Flag) or Sam si (Tricolor)
  • Adopted: December 2, 1975

Symbolism

    Colors:

  • Red: The blood shed for independence and the struggle for freedom
  • Blue: Prosperity, wealth, and the Mekong River
  • White: Unity, peace, and the bright future of the nation

    • Shapes / Symbols:

  • White disk: The moon over the Mekong River and the unity of the Lao people under one nation

Meaning / Special Display

  • The flag is widely displayed during National Day celebrations, symbolizing unity and national identity.
  • It is also raised at cultural and Buddhist events to reflect harmony and the peaceful character of Lao society.

Description

  • The flag represents Laos’ identity as a peaceful, unified nation rooted in tradition and community values.
  • Citizens associate the flag with simplicity, stability, and pride in their cultural heritage.
