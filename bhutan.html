Bhutan

Specifications

  • Local Name: འབྲུག་ཡུལ་ (Druk Yul)
  • Proportion: 2:3
  • Name of the Flag: འབྲུག་དར་ (Druk Dar) meaning “Thunder Dragon Flag”
  • Adopted: 1969

Symbolism

    Colors:

  • Yellow: The secular authority of the King and the nation’s civil tradition
  • Orange: The spiritual traditions of Buddhism, especially Drukpa Kagyu

    • Shapes / Symbols:

  • White dragon (Druk): Purity, protection, and the name of Bhutan itself (“Land of the Thunder Dragon”)
  • Dragon holding jewels: Prosperity and national wealth

Meaning / Special Display

  • Often displayed during national celebrations and religious festivals that reflect harmony between monarchy and spiritual heritage.
  • Treated with deep respect due to the dragon’s sacred symbolism within Bhutanese culture.

Description

  • The flag represents Bhutan’s unique balance between spiritual tradition and royal authority.
  • Citizens view it as a symbol of national identity, unity, and reverence for cultural heritage and the monarchy.
