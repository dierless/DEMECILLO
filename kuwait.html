Kuwait

Specifications

  • Local Name: الكويت (Al-Kuwait)
  • Proportion: 1:2
  • Name of the Flag: علم الكويت (Alam Kuwait) Alam Baladii (My Country's Flag) or Derti (My Homeland)
  • Adopted: September 7, 1961

Symbolism

    Colors:

  • Green: Fertility, hope, and the land
  • White: Peace and honorable intentions
  • Red: Bravery and sacrifice
  • Black: Past struggles and overcoming adversity

    • Shapes / Symbols:

  • Black trapezoid: Stability and protection of the nation

Meaning / Special Display

  • The flag is raised prominently at the Kuwait Towers during national celebrations, symbolizing modern identity and unity.
  • During Liberation Day events, citizens wave the flag to honor the country’s recovery and collective strength after the Gulf War.
  • Schools and public institutions host flag-raising ceremonies that emphasize patriotism and remembrance.

Description

  • The flag represents Kuwait’s resilience, national dignity, and its journey through difficulty toward peace and stability.
  • Its Pan-Arab colors connect the nation to a wider cultural heritage while expressing Kuwait’s independent identity.
