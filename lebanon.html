Lebanon

Specifications

  • Local Name: لبنان (Lubnān)
  • Proportion: 2:3
  • Name of the Flag: علم لبنان (Flag of the Kyrgyz Republic)
  • Adopted: December 7, 1943

Symbolism

    Colors:

  • Red: Sacrifice for freedom and the resilience of the Lebanese people
  • White: Peace, purity, and the snowy Mount Lebanon range

    • Shapes / Symbols:

  • Green Cedar Tree: Longevity, holiness, and the historic symbol of Lebanon’s identity

Meaning / Special Display

  • The cedar tree must always appear fully centered and never touching the red stripes, ensuring its symbolic purity and independence.
  • The flag is prominently displayed during Independence Day events, reflecting national unity and historical pride.

Description

  • The flag embodies Lebanon’s deep cultural heritage, symbolized by the ancient cedar that has represented the region for thousands of years.
  • For citizens, it reflects resilience, identity, and the enduring spirit of a nation shaped by history and diversity.
