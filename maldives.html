The Maldives

Specifications

  • Local Name: ދިވެހި ޖުމްހޫރިއްޔާ (Dhivehi Jumhooriyya)
  • Proportion: 2:3
  • Name of the Flag: Dhi’ruvaa (National Flag of the Maldives)
  • Adopted: July 25, 1965

Symbolism

    Colors:

  • Red: Sacrifice of the nation’s heroes
  • Green: Peace, prosperity, and Islamic faith
  • White: Purity and peace

    • Shapes / Symbols:

  • White crescent: Islam, the state religion
  • Rectangular red field with green rectangle and white crescent in the center

Meaning / Special Display

  • Displayed during national and religious events as a symbol of faith and unity.
  • Raised prominently on Independence Day (July 26) and Republic Day (November 11).

Description

  • The flag reflects the Maldives’ Islamic heritage, national pride, and the sacrifices of its people.
  • For citizens, it embodies unity, peace, and respect for tradition.
